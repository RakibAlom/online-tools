Ramadan Mubarak vs. Ramadan Kareem: What to Write

A common question for our free online ramadan mubarak greeting card creator tool users is whether to use "Ramadan Mubarak" or "Ramadan Kareem." "Ramadan Mubarak" means "Blessed Ramadan" and is universally accepted and warmly received by Muslims everywhere. "Ramadan Kareem" means "Generous Ramadan" and while widely used, some Islamic scholars prefer "Mubarak" as it attributes blessings to Allah rather than to the month itself. Our card generator defaults to "Ramadan Mubarak" but allows you to customize the title to whichever greeting you prefer, including Arabic script if you wish to type it directly.
