Tips for Creating the Perfect Get Well Soon Card

Match the tone to the situation. A humorous card works beautifully for a close friend with a seasonal cold but would be deeply inappropriate for someone receiving a serious diagnosis. Read the situation and choose a design and message accordingly.

Personalize beyond just the name. The name is the minimum level of personalization. For maximum impact, reference something specific — a shared memory, a particular quality you admire in them, or a specific hope for their recovery ("I can't wait to hike the mountain together when you're fully well").

Choose the right size for your sharing method. For WhatsApp sharing, Standard or Story size works best. For Instagram posts, Square is optimal. For printing, choose any size and use PDF export. For email, JPG format offers the best balance of quality and file size.

Let the design do some of the emotional work. A carefully chosen design communicates mood and tone before a single word is read. A warm, heart-filled design says "this comes with love." An elegant, refined design says "I respect you deeply." Let the visual and verbal work together.

Don't overthink the message. Genuine simplicity beats elaborate insincerity. "I care about you deeply and I'm thinking of you every day" is more meaningful than a lengthy, performative message that rings hollow. Speak from your heart in whatever words come naturally.

Consider sending more than one. A card at the start of an illness, one midway through a long recovery, and one celebrating their return to full health creates a sense of sustained care that is truly memorable and meaningful.
