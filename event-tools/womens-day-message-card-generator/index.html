International Women's Day History and Significance

International Women's Day traces its roots to 1908 when 15,000 women marched through New York City demanding shorter hours, better pay, and voting rights. The first National Woman's Day was observed in the United States on February 28, 1909. In 1910, an International Women's Conference led to the establishment of International Women's Day, first honored in 1911 in Austria, Denmark, Germany, and Switzerland.

Today, March 8th is celebrated globally with different themes each year focusing on various aspects of women's rights, achievements, and ongoing struggles for equality.
